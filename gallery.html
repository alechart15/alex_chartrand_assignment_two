Color of Evil \ Color of Innocence

In the show, red does not necessarily mean "evil" or "innocent" directly, but relates more-so to emotional vulnerability, and the exploitation (evil) or acceptance (innocence) of it. We see red in the outfits of Sachiko, Airi, and Kayo, denoting the openness that allows Satoru to be vulnerable with each of them. The eyes of many characters flash red throughout the show, indicating high levels of emotion, whether positive or negative.

Position of Power

The shows main theme of child abuse sheds light on the power dynamics between adults and children, and often employs the use of camera angles in order to reinforce this theme. Adults often loom over the children, a super-imposing presence that can either comfort, or instill fear.

Views of Childhood

Another brilliant cinematographic feat of the show is the use of differing aspect ratios depending on whether Satoru is a child or adult. When Satoru returns to the past, the show begins to use the 2.35:1 aspect ratio, adding a set of black bars to the top and bottom of the screen. This change reflects Satoru’s now smaller body, as well as the claustrophobia and helplessness he feels as a child.
